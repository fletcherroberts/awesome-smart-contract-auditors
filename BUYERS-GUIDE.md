# How to Choose a Smart Contract Audit Company

If you are comparing **smart contract audit companies**, **blockchain security firms**, or the **best smart contract auditors** for a live Web3 project, this guide is for you.

## What actually matters when choosing a smart contract auditor

Most teams should evaluate firms on seven factors:

1. **Manual review quality**
2. **Experience in your exact stack**
3. **Clarity of findings and reports**
4. **Remediation support**
5. **Timeline realism**
6. **Public research credibility**
7. **Commercial fit for your team**

## Smart contract audit checklist

Use this checklist before hiring any auditor:

- Who will actually review the code?
- Have they audited similar protocols before?
- How much of the audit is manual versus tool assisted?
- What is included in remediation and retesting?
- How are findings prioritized?
- What is the expected turnaround time?
- Can they explain the process clearly to both founders and engineers?
- Do they have relevant research, reports, or public proof of capability?

## Questions to ask a smart contract auditing firm

### 1. Who is on the audit team?
The logo matters less than the specific reviewers.

### 2. What similar systems have you reviewed?
Look for real experience with DeFi, bridges, staking systems, rollups, or whatever matches your architecture.

### 3. What happens after findings are delivered?
Strong remediation support saves time and reduces risk.

### 4. How do you handle scope changes?
Protocols often move fast. You want a firm that can handle real delivery conditions.

### 5. How much of the work is manual?
A good audit uses tools, but it should not rely on tools alone.
